Problem 1

Find the smallest n such that 2013n ends in 001.

Solution

It is easy to prove that the problem is equivalent to finding the smallest n>0 such that 2013n≡1(mod1000).

2013n≡1(mod1000)​⟺2013n≡1(mod23)∧2013n≡1(mod53)⟺5n≡1(mod23)∧13n≡1(mod53)⟺23∣5n−1n∧53∣13n−1n⟺ν2​(5n−1n)≥3∧ν5​(13n−1n)≥3​​

If n≡0(mod2), applying the Lifting-the-Exponent Lemma yields

ν2​(5n−1n)​=ν2​(4)+ν2​(6)+ν2​(n)−1=2+1+ν2​(n)−1≥3​​

If n≡1(mod2), applying the Lifting-the-Exponent Lemma yields

ν2​(5n−1n)​=ν2​(4)=2<3​​

Therefore,

ν2​(5n−1n)≥3⟺2∣n ν5​(13n−1n)≥3​⟹3n≡1(mod5)⟺δ5​(3)∣n⟺4∣n​​

Therefore,

ν5​(13n−1n)≥3​⟺4∣n∧ν5​(13n−1n)≥3⟺∃k∈Z,n=4k∧ν5​(13n−1n)≥3⟺∃k∈Z,n=4k∧ν5​((134)k−(14)k)≥3​​

Applying the Lifting-the-Exponent Lemma yields

ν5​((134)k−(14)k)​=ν5​(134−14)+ν5​(k)=ν5​(13−1)+ν5​(13+1)+ν5​(132+12)+ν5​(k)=1+ν5​(k)​​

Therefore,

ν5​(13n−1n)≥3​⟺∃k∈Z,n=4k∧1+ν5​(k)≥3⟺∃k∈Z,n=4k∧25∣k⟺100∣n​​

Therefore,

2013n≡1(mod1000)​⟺2∣n∧100∣n⟺100∣n​​

The smallest n>0 such that 100∣n is 100.

Problem 2

Let n be the least positive integer such that 149n−2n is divisible by 335577. Find the number of positive divisors of n.

Solution

335577∣(149n−2n)​⟺33∣149n−2n∧55∣149n−2n∧77∣149n−2n⟺ν3​(149n−2n)≥3∧ν5​(149n−2n)≥5∧ν7​(149n−2n)≥7​​

ν3​(149n−2n)​=ν3​(147)+ν3​(n)=1+ν3​(n)​​

ν3​(149n−2n)≥3​⟺1+ν3​(n)≥3⟺32∣n​​ ν5​(149n−2n)≥5⟹149n≡2n(mod5)

Since

149n≡{1(mod5),4(mod5),​n≡0(mod2)n≡1(mod2)​ 2n≡⎩⎨⎧​1(mod5),2(mod5),4(mod5),3(mod5),​n≡0(mod4)n≡1(mod4)n≡2(mod4)n≡3(mod4)​

it follows that

149n≡2n(mod5)⟺4∣n

Therefore,

ν5​(149n−2n)≥5​⟺4∣n∧ν5​(149n−2n)≥5⟺∃k∈Z,n=4k∧ν5​(149n−2n)≥5⟺∃k∈Z,n=4k∧ν5​((1494)n−(24)n)≥5​​

Applying the Lifting-the-Exponent Lemma yields

ν7​(149n−2n)​=ν7​(147)+ν7​(n)=2+ν7​(n)​​

Therefore,

ν7​(149n−2n)≥7​⟺2+ν7​(n)≥7⟺75∣n​​

Therefore,

335577∣149n−2n​⟺32∣n∧4⋅54∣n∧75∣n⟺22325475∣n​​

The smallest n>0 such that 22325475∣n is 22325475, it has (2+1)(2+1)(4+1)(5+1)=270 divisors.

Problem 6

Let k be a positive integer. Find all positive integers n such that 3k∣2n−1.

Solution

3k∣2n−1​⟹3∣2n−1⟺2n≡1(mod3)⟺δ3​(2)∣n⟺2∣n​​

Therefore,

3k∣2n−1​⟺2∣n∧3k∣2n−1⟺∃m∈Z,n=2m∧3k∣2n−1⟺∃m∈Z,n=2m∧3k∣4m−1⟺∃m∈Z,n=2m∧ν3​(4m−1m)≥k​​

ν3​(4m−1m)​=ν3​(4−1)+ν3​(m)=1+ν3​(m)​​

Problem 7

Find all primes p,q such that

pq(5p−2p)(5q−2q)​

is an integer.

Solution

Wlog, assume p≤q.

pq(5p−2p)(5q−2q)​∈Z⟺pq∣(5p−2p)(5q−2q)

Since

(5p−2p)(5q−2q)≡1(mod2)

it follows that

pq∣(5p−2p)(5q−2q)⟹p=2∧q=2 pq∣(5p−2p)(5q−2q)​⟹p∣(5p−2p)(5q−2q)⟺(5p−2p)(5q−2q)≡0(modp)​​

Applying Fermat’s Little Theorem yields

5p−2p≡3(modp)

Therefore, if p=3,

(5p−2p)(5q−2q)≡0(modp)​⟺5q−2q≡0(modp)⟺(25​)q≡1(modp)⟺δp​(25​)∣q⟺δp​(25​)=q​​

Applying Fermat’s Little Theorem yields

(25​)p−1≡1(modp) (25​)p−1≡1(modp)⟺δp​(25​)∣p−1

Therefore,

(5p−2p)(5q−2q)≡0(modp)​⟹q∣p−1⟹q<p​​

Therefore,

pq∣(5p−2p)(5q−2q)​⟺39(5q−2q)≡0(modq)⟺q∣117⟺q=3∨q=13​​

Therefore, all primes p,q such that
